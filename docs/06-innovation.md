# 06 — Innovation: What Competitors Don't Have

Six features in this build exist nowhere in the competitive set (doc 01). Each is grounded in the transcripts and the gap analysis.

## 1. Coach-Verified AI Video Review (`/coach/video`)
**What:** AI takes the first pass on session video (observations with confidence scores); the coach confirms or dismisses each finding; only coach-approved analysis ever reaches the parent and player portals, delivered with the coach's own comment.
**Why it matters:** The T2 parent interview was unambiguous — "I don't trust AI as much as I would a biomechanics coach." Research confirmed the pattern market-wide: consumer AI verdicts (Mustard, Sportsbox) earn "get a real coach" reactions, while coach-mediated tools (OnForm) win trust. Nobody has fused the AI first-pass with the facility's own coach inside one approval workflow. This is Jim's AI mandate delivered in the only form parents will buy.

## 2. Quality Reps (`/player/reps`, surfaced in `/parent/wellness`)
**What:** Players log practice reps with a focus tag and a 1–5 quality self-rating; the system reports average quality per focus and coaches volume *down* when quality sags ("drop to 20 reps and make them count").
**Why it matters:** "Quality reps instead of just quantity reps" (T2) is a coaching philosophy no product operationalizes — every competitor counts volume (DK swing counts, Rapsodo session counts). This is the development-culture moat in software form, and it feeds the over-training guard (#3).

## 3. Workload Guard / Wellness Tracking (`/parent/wellness`)
**What:** Weekly sleep, nutrition, soreness, and throwing-workload tracking against coach-set caps, with visual warnings as load approaches the limit.
**Why it matters:** The parent persona's stated priorities — "health, sleep, eating, the right trainers, not overworking yourself" (T2) — are served by zero baseball platforms. Arm-care workload caps tied to the facility's actual session schedule is a genuinely new, safety-first surface that gives parents a reason to open the portal weekly.

## 4. Diamond Dollars — Cross-Location Rewards (`/player/rewards`, `/parent/shop`, `/owner/rewards`, `/hq/rewards`)
**What:** Points earned on any spend at any Frozen Ropes facility, redeemable at any Pro Shop in the network; tier-multiplied (Membership Matrix); streak bonuses reward practice consistency, not just spend.
**Why it matters:** Tony's exact ask (T1 00:24:39) — and structurally unavailable to competitors: GameChanger/TeamSnap have no retail, Upper Hand is single-facility-minded, and no youth-sports product has a network loyalty layer. The HQ view shows earn/redeem corridors between cities — the network-as-moat story for investors.

## 5. The Blueprint Simulator (`/sneak-peek`, with the live Blueprint at `/owner/blueprint`)
**What:** The prospect-facing surface turns the license pitch into an interactive economics model (members × fees × cage hours → revenue/EBITDA from Blueprint benchmarks), and the owner-facing Blueprint tracks real P&L against those same benchmarks line-by-line.
**Why it matters:** Competitors sell software; Frozone sells a *business in a box*, and this makes that tangible in 30 seconds. It operationalizes the "sneak peek to convince people to write a check" requirement (T1 00:09:21) and the only sales asset Sal said was timeline-critical. No facility-software vendor has a prospect-facing simulator.

## 6. Cradle-to-Grave Journey (`/player/journey` + College Bound at `/parent/recruiting`)
**What:** A player-visible arc from Born to Play (age 3) to College Bound (D1/D2/D3 fit matching on academics + verified Rock Ready data), with the recruiting database positioned as the honest anti-showcase: "know where you stand before you pay Perfect Game $750."
**Why it matters:** "Cradle to grave… start the relationship when you are a young parent and evolve into the college process" (T1 00:16:26). Perfect Game and PBR monetize anxiety event-by-event; CaptainU is disconnected from daily training. Longitudinal facility-verified data matched to academic fit is a retention engine (families can't leave without losing the record) and a resentment-powered marketing wedge documented in doc 01 §2.5.
